Our mission is to relieve hardship and support people in need — with a particular focus on disadvantaged migrants settling in Sydney, and on helping them rebuild their working lives.

Who we are

Multicultural Communities Support Center Inc is a Sydney-based not-for-profit, established in 2026, providing practical and culturally appropriate help to disadvantaged members of the global communities settled in Australia, and to others in need.

Real support happens on the ground, through direct human connection and practical help. We were established by people who saw the hurdles new arrivals and vulnerable community members face — and decided to do something about it. A particular focus of ours is employment: helping skilled migrants return to meaningful employment in their area of expertise, and helping international students build career foundations while they study.

Assistance is provided freely and on the basis of assessed need, to people who are unable to adequately support themselves without intervention, and whose needs arouse compassion in the community.

Our vision

A safe, inclusive and supportive community where vulnerable individuals and families are protected from harm, connected to essential services, and supported to live with dignity and independence.

Our mission

To relieve hardship, distress and suffering by providing practical, compassionate and culturally appropriate support to people in need — with a particular focus on disadvantaged migrants in Sydney.

What guides us

Our values

  • CompassionWe treat every person's situation with respect, empathy and dignity.
  • Cultural respectWe embrace and support people from all backgrounds, in language and ways that make sense to them.
  • Practical helpWe focus on real-world solutions that directly improve daily lives.
  • EmpowermentWe give people the tools, knowledge and confidence to navigate systems independently.

Acknowledgement of Country

Multicultural Communities Support Center Inc acknowledges the Traditional Custodians of the lands on which we live and work, and pays respect to Elders past, present and emerging. We recognise their enduring connection to Country, and we are committed to walking alongside all communities — First Nations and newly arrived alike — with respect and dignity.
