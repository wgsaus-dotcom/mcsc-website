Who we are

Standing with people in need, from day one

Arriving in a new country, or facing hardship in an unfamiliar system, is hard to do alone. We were established in 2026 to offer hands-on, culturally appropriate help to disadvantaged members of the global communities settled in Australia.

Support is offered freely, on the basis of assessed need — to people who cannot adequately support themselves without it, and whose needs arouse compassion in the community.

More about us

Our purpose

To relieve hardship, distress and suffering through practical, compassionate and culturally appropriate support to people in need — with a particular focus on disadvantaged migrants in Sydney.

What we do

Programs directed to genuine need

Each program is delivered by trained staff and volunteers with the right language and cultural capability, with referrals to specialist services.

Our focus

Employment in your area of expertise

Helping skilled migrants get back into their area of expertise — not just any job.

Migrant settlement

Practical help with housing, essential services and daily life in those first crucial months.

International students

Guidance, referrals, internships and job-readiness support while you study in Australia.

Digital skills for seniors

Patient, friendly sessions so older people stay connected with family and services.

Health, disability & aged care

Support and referrals for people affected by illness, a pandemic, disability or age — incl. NDIS.

Family & crisis support

Confidential, trauma-informed support and referrals for people in distress.

JP & Notary services

Free Justice of the Peace witnessing and certification, plus Notary services by appointment — helping you get documents witnessed, certified or notarised for services, study, employment and overseas use.

Our flagship focus

Skilled migrants, working in their area of expertise again

Too many people arrive with real qualifications — nurses, engineers, accountants, teachers, trades — and get stuck in survival jobs far below their training. We exist to change that.

We walk alongside each person from first résumé to first day at work, and help international students build a career foundation while they study.

The barriers we break

  • Overseas qualifications not recognised by local employers
  • No local work history or professional references
  • Résumés & interview styles that don't match Australian norms
  • No professional network to hear about roles
  • Licensing, registration & bridging steps that feel impossible
  • Language & cultural confidence at interview

Acknowledgement of Country

Multicultural Communities Support Center Inc acknowledges the Traditional Custodians of the lands on which we live and work, and pays respect to Elders past, present and emerging.
